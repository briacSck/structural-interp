# Microfounded zoo v2 — annotated readings (run of 2026-07-06, n = 13)

Raw numbers: `zoo_v2_summary_raw.md` / `zoo_v2_rows.csv` (crc32 seeding,
reproducible). Headline Spearman with CF RMSE: battery 0.16, c-channel −0.01,
x-channel 0.53. The n=5 "perfect battery" did NOT survive scaling — and the
reason is the paper's best finding so far.

## Finding 1 — Structural estimation is ROBUST to in-class bounded rationality

The Gabaix sparse-max and Matějka–McKay RI agents — including the *fully
regime-blind* sparse_m0.0 — produce near-zero counterfactual failure
(CF RMSE 0.005–0.008, on par with the clone). Why: attention attenuation
maps into the payoff parameter. NFXP fits sparse_m0.0 with θ̂₂ = 0.005 and
R̂C = 10.05 — it correctly recovers the agent's *effective* (as-if)
preferences, and the regime-shift counterfactual is then genuinely correct
*for that agent* (it does not respond to regimes, and the model says so).
θ̂₂ tracks the attention parameter across both families (m: 0.005→0.337;
λ: 0.131→0.540). This is the Fosgerau et al. (2020) equivalence made
empirical: RI-logit attenuation stays INSIDE the logit model class, so the
estimator absorbs it into parameters and transport survives.

**Consequence: the c-use audit does not — and should not — predict failure**
(Spearman −0.01). "Uses the regime less" is not a specification error; it is
in-class preference heterogeneity.

## Finding 2 — What kills counterfactuals is OUT-OF-CLASS cognition, and the
## lookup-ness audit sees it

Within the Fryer–Jackson categorical family, the x-dispersion audit orders
counterfactual failure PERFECTLY (n = 6, Spearman 1.00):

| granularity | audit_x | CF RMSE |
|---|---|---|
| cat4x1 | 1.148 | 0.218 |
| cat6x1 | 1.025 | 0.118 |
| cat6x2 | 0.815 | 0.107 |
| cat8x2 | 0.769 | 0.075 |
| cat12x3 | 0.664 | 0.023 |
| cat16x5 | 0.528 | 0.017 |

Categorical cognition is NOT in the logit class (piecewise-constant CCPs are
not generated by any (θ₁, θ₂, RC)); the estimator projects it onto the class
and the projection error surfaces as biased R̂C (4.6–7.0 vs 10) and failed
transport. The salience-asymmetric agents sit in between (out-of-class in the
c-dimension only; CF ≈ 0.023–0.026), which the x-audit partially misses —
consistent with "each audit sees its own channel."

## The paper's sharpened thesis

> Structural counterfactuals are robust to bounded rationality that lives
> inside the model class (inattention à la Gabaix/Matějka–McKay: absorbed
> into as-if parameters, transport survives) and silently fail for cognition
> outside it (categorization à la Fryer–Jackson: projected onto wrong deep
> parameters, transport breaks). Internal audits therefore should not test
> *which variables the agent uses* but *whether its internal mechanism
> conforms to the assumed model class* — and a causal lookup-ness audit
> (dispersion of local sensitivity) does exactly that, ordering failure
> perfectly within the categorical family.

## Methodological notes
- LEACE amnesic audit validated (post-erasure probe R² ≈ 0; 56× clone vs
  c-blind separation) — it is the right tool for "does the agent use Z",
  which turned out to be the wrong question for failure prediction.
- Battery-as-max normalization across heterogeneous families dilutes signal;
  the model-class-conformity audit (x-dispersion, possibly extended to
  c-dimension steps for the asym agents) is the object to develop.
